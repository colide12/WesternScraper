              Synopsis

              Background: Inventor applied for patent on system for identifying and transporting portable memory devices with three color-coded components of labels, attachment members, such as key rings, and wristbands. The United States Patent and Trademark Office, Patent Trial and Appeal Board, 2016 WL 4579854, denied patent as obvious. Applicant appealed.
              Holdings: The Court of Appeals held that:
              1 claim that required marking labels with pre-set words or pre-set symbols was obvious in light of prior art that disclosed use of pre-determined words even without reference to symbols;
              2 labels relating to contents of memory devices labeled, not to labels on which they were printed, were not entitled to any patentable weight;
              3 Board could rely on prior art for use of color-coded system in conjunction with memory devices in denying application for patent as obvious; and
              4 substantial evidence supported finding motivation to combine prior art references to find that patent was obvious.
              Affirmed.
